Foods commonly eaten:

Breakfast

For the most part food on the breakfast meanu is the same are here. However, there are some special foods that are amazingly delicious breakfast foods that all Ethiopians enjoy. Some common foods are chechebsa, shown above, it is shreded tortilla cooked with some spicy sauces.

Lunch/Dinner

Ethiopian lunch/diner cuisine mostly consistis of vegetables and often very spicy meat dishes. If you don't want the spicy foods then you can specify to whom ever is serving that you only the non-spicy stew. The stews are called "wat",served on top of or with injera, a large sourdough flatbread. It is made out of fermented teff flour, it's about 50cm (20 inches). Ethiopians usually eat with their right hand, using a small piece of injera to pick up bite-sized entrees and side dishes, although utensils are an option. If you are vegan and/or vegetarian no need to worry,since the Ethiopian Orthodox Church has fasting on most Wednesdays and Fridays and some fasting days there are a lot of vegan/vegetarian options.
If you have any allergy or health conserns you can check in and ask the resturant you are eating at.

Meeting Etiquettes

  • A handshake and direct eye contact is the most common form of greeting.
  • After a close person relationship, friends of the same sex may kiss on check three times.
  • Men should wait to see if a woman extends her hand.
  • Greeting are never rushed. Take your time talk about the person's family,health, job, life, etc.
  • "Ato", "Woizero", and "Woizrit" are used to referr to/ address a man, married woman, and unmarried woman respectively.
  • Always greet elders first.
  • It is customary to bow when being intriduced to someone who is older or has a more senior position. Children will often do this.

Before you go...

Ivory is illegal:

Travelers transporting ivory may be detained, imprisoned, or fined and the ivory may be confiscated.

Currency:

The Ethiopian Birr (ETB) is the currency in Ethiopia, with the exception of international hotel and airport bills, payment for commercial transactions in any other currency is illegal. Credit Cards are accepted at only a very few outlets in Addis Ababa.

Crime:

Pick-pocketing, purse snatching, thesft from vehicles and other petty crimes are common in Addis Ababa. Especially be careful on Bole Road and in the Piazza, the Merkato Market, and other areas frequently visited by tourists and foreigners.


No Photos of Government Building, Military Installations, Police/Military personnel and key infrastructure such as roads, bridges, dams, and airfields...

It is illegal to take pictures of government buildings, military installations, police/military personnel and key infrastructure such as roads, bridges, dams, and airfields. If caught you could be fined, your photographic equipment could be confiscated, and you could be detained and/or arrested. For general practice, it is advised to avoid taking pictures of individuals without their clear conesent.

Cell Phone Service:

Cellphone services are unreliable and landlines are nearly non-existent. It may be possible to purchase a SIM card locally and use a U.S-compatible cell phone. As mentioned before, cellphone access may be cut off without warning.

Climate

During Decemebr in Addis Ababa the weather is very nice. The average temperature is mild at 15°C (59°F). Afternoon can go up to an average high temperature of 23°C (73.4°F). During the night it gets a little cool down to an average low of 7°C (44.6°F).

Enviroment

Altitudes may be a problem for some travels going to Ethiopia. Addis Ababa is located more than 7,000 feet above sea level. Especially those who suffer from respiratory or heart problems.

Requirements for Entry:

  • Passport
  • Visa- Travel visas are available for tourist at Addis Ababa (Bole airport), for about $50 for 1 month. Although all other categories of visitor must get a visa from the Ethiopian Embassy closest to their place of legal residence before travelling.
  • World Health Organization (WHO) cards with vaccinations

Carried Medications

If you are carring prescribed medicine, carry it in it's original packing with your doctors prescription.

Vaccination before trip

The CDC and WHO recommend and/or require the following vaccines for traveling to Ethiopia: typhoid, yellow fever, hepatitis A, hepatitis B, meningitis, polio, rabies, measles, mumps, rubella(MMR), and influenza.

Animals

There aren't any wild animals you should be especially careful of but there could be possiblities of running into some wild dogs and bats.
